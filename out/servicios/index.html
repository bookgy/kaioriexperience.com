How we work

A clear, direct process focused on real improvements.

01. We understand the business

We review the type of guest, main channels, goals and the points the business wants to improve.

02. We analyse as real guests

We search, compare, review, book or simulate the process and experience the journey from the guest's perspective.

03. We detect friction

We identify doubts, inconsistencies, clarity issues, mismanaged expectations and details that affect perception.

04. We prioritise findings

We classify each point by impact on trust, conversion, satisfaction and reputation.

05. We deliver a clear plan

We turn the analysis into concrete actions: what to improve, in what order and with what objective.

06. We support improvement

Optionally, we review applied changes and help adjust the experience over time.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is this only for hotels?⌄

No. We work with accommodation, hotels, restaurants, tours, experiences and service businesses where customer experience directly influences decisions, satisfaction and recommendation.

Is the audit digital or on site?⌄

It can be digital, on site or complete. The digital audit analyses search, website, platforms, reputation, booking and communication. Mystery Guest adds the real service experience from within.

Do I need a lot of internal data?⌄

Not necessarily. Kaiori starts with the real customer experience and publicly available information. If the business has internal metrics, they can enrich the analysis.

What do I receive at the end?⌄

A clear report with findings, evidence, diagnosis and a prioritised action plan with concrete improvements.

Does this replace a marketing agency?⌄

No. Kaiori is not a traditional marketing agency. We help understand and improve the real customer experience. That insight can later support marketing, conversion, operations and reputation.

Do you also review ratings and comments?⌄

Yes. We analyse reviews and reputation patterns to detect recurring problems, real strengths and improvement points.

What kind of improvements do you propose?⌄

Digital, operational, communication, experience and reputation improvements. Always prioritised by impact and ease of implementation.
