The problem

The real experience does not always match what the business believes it offers.

A business can have good photos, a correct website and positive reviews, yet still accumulate small details that only appear when someone lives the full experience.

Details normalised by the team

What the business sees every day can go unnoticed internally, while strongly affecting the guest experience.

Expectations that do not match

When what is communicated before the visit does not match what is experienced, disappointment, doubts and negative reviews appear.

Invisible critical moments

Arrival, waiting, cleanliness, service or closing can strengthen trust or weaken it.

Reviews that could be avoided

Many negative reviews come from small frictions that could be detected and corrected earlier.

We observe every point of the real customer journey, from what guests expect before arriving to what they remember afterwards.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about Mystery Guest

Is a Mystery Guest audit a real guest visit?⌄

Yes. The audit is based on experiencing the service from the guest's point of view. Depending on the business, it may include booking, arrival, use of the service, attention, cleanliness, closing and later analysis.

Is it a secret or announced visit?⌄

It can be designed in different ways depending on the case. In some services it makes sense to experience it naturally as a real guest; in others, it can be coordinated in advance with the business. We define this before the audit.

What type of businesses can request it?⌄

Hotels, vacation rentals, restaurants, tours, experiences, spas, travel services and businesses where the lived experience directly affects satisfaction, reputation and recommendation.

Where will it be available?⌄

The next route includes Australia, New Zealand, Oceania, Pacific Islands and French Polynesia. Availability depends on dates, exact location and business fit.

Does it also include digital analysis?⌄

Yes. Before the visit, we review the business's basic digital presence to understand what expectation is created before arrival: website, platforms, Google profile, photos, reviews and public information.

What do I receive afterwards?⌄

You receive a report with findings, evidence, impact diagnosis and prioritised recommendations to improve the real customer experience.

Will you publish a review or content about my business?⌄

Not necessarily. Kaiori is not a promotional visit or a content collaboration. The goal is to analyse the experience and help the business improve. Any public use of content would be agreed separately.

Is the initial diagnosis free?⌄

Yes. You can request a free first assessment so we can review whether your business fits the Mystery Guest audit and our planned route.

Does the Mystery Guest audit have a cost?⌄

Yes. Mystery Guest is a professional service. The initial diagnosis helps assess the case, define scope and confirm whether it makes sense to prepare a proposal.

Can it be done outside those areas?⌄

Yes, but it depends on agenda, travel and feasibility. Current priority will be Australia, New Zealand, Oceania, Pacific Islands and French Polynesia.
